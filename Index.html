ABOUT

Hi there! I'm Yeva, a multimedia graphic design student from Ukraine, based in Arnhem (Netherlands), currently studying at ArtEZ Academy. I love researching and working with bold, loud ideas - my work dives into political and cultural themes that make people think and feel something. I'm drawn to topics that are often personal but expand into wider social conversations - things that might feel uncomfortable or heavy, but are important to address.
I believe graphic design can be a voice for these stories, translating personal experiences into visual language that resonates universally. I don't like to put myself in borders, which is exactly why multimedia design feels right for me. I'm always open to learning different techniques - whether that means getting my hands dirty with analog processes or diving into complicated new programs.
I approach design through a cultural lens, creating visuals that go beyond looking good, and I'm constantly experimenting across both traditional and digital methods to discover unexpected solutions. My process is all about that blend of exploration and intention. I believe design should spark conversations, challenge perspectives, and leave a lasting impression.
